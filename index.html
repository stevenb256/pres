Reality Check

AI is not a normal platform shift

It’s non-deterministic, fast-moving, and wonderfully weird.

Traditional shift

  • Mostly deterministic math
  • Incremental validation
  • Clear end-user value patterns
  • Predictable competition

AI shift

  • Non-stochastic → stochastic mindset
  • Harder to test & validate at scale
  • Requires serious experimentation muscle
  • Search for end-user value in new spaces
  • Incredible competitive velocity (fastest 0→1’s ever)
  • Early days are bumpy — that’s normal

Analogy: We went from building trains to training cats. Both move fast — only one ignores your instructions.

Experience

What we’ve learned in the last two years

Short version: AI is powerful, but honest evals are even more powerful.

RAG ≠ magic

Retrieval quality is queen; grounding is king. No grounding, no crown.

Evals matter

We ship what we measure. Good evals turn opinions into signal.

Models have vibes

Genius… and confidently wrong. That’s why safety rails and UX matter.

Context is finite

Windows & KV caches are budgets. Spend them like product people.

New stacks

Sydney, Flux, Bizchat, semantic search — choose boring when you can, new when you must.

Everything is an agent

Copilots everywhere. The trick is orchestration, not sprawl.

Day to Day

Using AI to do our jobs

Think of AI as a team of tireless, very literal interns.

Coding

  • You’re managing agents, not just code
  • Ask for variants, compare, compose
  • Treat prompts like code: version, review, reuse

Debugging

  • Triage with LLMs + traces
  • Generate hypotheses quickly
  • Keep the human-in-the-loop

PM & UX

  • Prototype interactions live on real content
  • Eval with real tasks, not vibes
  • Ship narratives, not slide decks (ironic, we know)

Intermission

Frequently Blurted Questions

Because someone was going to ask these anyway.

Is AI going to take my job?

No. But the person who learns to wield AI effectively might. Let’s make that person you.

What if the model is confidently wrong?

Cool, then it’s human. That’s why we ground answers, show citations, and keep Undo close.

Can we ship without perfect evals?

We can ship without perfect anything. But we can’t improve what we don’t measure.
